1. Liar's Paradox


The statement of the Liar's Paradox is -



"This sentence is false"

The sentence itself is a paradox. Here everything is about the perception of words true and false. In the above statement "This sentence is false" is true then the statement is indeed false. That is if we see the statement as a whole it is true. But speaking the content of the statement, what it implies is false.This is what we call as Liar's Paradox. The antinomy of the liar is the statement of a liar that he or she is lying: for instance, declaring that "I am lying". If the liar is indeed lying, then the liar is telling the truth, which means the liar just lied

2. Grandfather Axe Paradox


The Grandfather Axe Paradox states that -



If you have your grandfather's axe. After using it for some time its head is chirped. So you replace its head with a new one. After some time its handle is broken so you replace the handle as well. Now as the entire object is replaced piece by piece does it truly the same object as in the question.?

It is also called as ship of theseus paradox. It is a thought experiment that raises the question of whether an object that has had all of its components replaced remains fundamentally the same object. The concept is one of the oldest in Western philosophy, having been discussed by the likes of Heraclitus and Plato by ca. 500–400 BC.



I personally think that the answer to the paradox is NO. The object doesn't truly remain the same it was earlier. Because by replacing the object you are also removing the properties that the piece of object possessed before it was replaced. Say the head of the before replacing had 80% Iron and 20% copper then the replaced part may have 70% Iron and 30% Copper. So it truly doesn't remain the same object.

3. The Twin Paradox

The twin paradox says that -



There are 2 twin brothers A and B. A goes on a spaceship what revolves around the earth at the speed near to speed of light and the other twin B stays on earth. After 5 years when A returns on Earth he sees that he has aged only 5 years but everyone on earth including the other twin B is aged 65 years.

This is called as Twin Paradox. The concept of time changes for both the twins. In A's frame of reference the time is normal for A but when B views it from his frame of reference he sees that the time is moving faster for A. This is also an illustration of the world famous equation e=mc2
which is the Equation of General Theory of Relativity.

5. Zeno's Paradox


Zeno's paradoxes are a set of philosophical problems generally thought to have been devised by Greek philosopher Zeno of Elea (c. 490–430 BC)

The Paradoxes under zeno's paradox are as follows

  • Arrow Paradox -
    "Consider a arrow moving forward. At any instance of time, the arrow can't go where it isn't because the time required for that movement is not elapsed yet. And the arrow can't go where it is. Because its already there."


  • Dichotomy paradox -
    "Suppose Atalanta wishes to walk to the end of a path. Before she can get there, she must get halfway there. Before she can get halfway there, she must get a quarter of the way there. Before traveling a quarter, she must travel one-eighth; before an eighth, one-sixteenth; and so on."


  • Achilles and the tortoise -
    "In a race, the quickest runner can never over­take the slowest, since the pursuer must first reach the point whence the pursued started, so that the slower must always hold a lead."


The comman feature of all thease paradoxes is that they eventually say that Time is truly motionless and if time is composed of instance then motion is an impossibility. So basically it says Motion is an illusion.
